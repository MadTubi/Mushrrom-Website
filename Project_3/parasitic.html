Parasitic-Feeding on the Weak

Parasitic types of mushrooms also take plant hosts. Although in this case, the relationship is one-sided. These fungi will infect the host and eventually kill it.

Sometimes the line between parasitic and saprotrophic is not so clear. The honey mushroom is a known parasite yet it will also continue to live saprotrophically on the dead wood of its host. Most true parasitic fungi do not produce mushrooms and are too small to be noticed on a tree until itâ€™s too late.

  • Caterpillar Fungus
  • Lion's Mane
  • Chaga
